These are some personal galleries that I’ve implemented using Adobe Portfolio rather than embed them in a website page. There were two reasons for this. Firstly, the software that I use to create the blog isn’t really gallery-friendly and it’s a lot of hassle to embed galleries in a page; secondly, Portfolio is integrated with Lightroom. Galleries can therefore be created and updated in Lightroom and seamlessly uploaded to the Portfolio site.

However, in keeping with my belief that words and pictures go together, all of the images are captioned and not simply titled.

The galleries that are available on Portfolio are:

  1. A Year in Braidburn Park. A wee project where I selected 6 photos illustrating the park, which is close to where we live, for each month from August 2021 to July 2022.

  2. The River Dee - from source to sea. A long-term project which I started when we lived in Aberdeen to illustrate the River Dee. Sadly, I haven’t yet managed to get to the source in the Cairngorms but I live in hope.

  3. Untourist Edinburgh. A project where I have tried to illustrate aspects of Edinburgh that are not on the normal tourist track. This isn’t about gritty housing schemes but about some of the attractive but less visited places in Edinburgh or different perspectives on familiar sights.

  4. Experiments - my most recent and continuing project where I am experimenting with different processing techniques to create a more impressionistic view of the world. I explain the motivation for this in my thoughts on being Disenchanted with Digital (link).
